Gas Station Cyberpunk

A nighttime gas station scene with over 2 million primitives, ray traced from scratch in C++.

Scene Overview

From Blender to Rays

The scene depicts a moody, nighttime gas station featuring a BMW-style sports car and a motorcycle parked under the canopy. The entire environment was modeled in Blender, exported as an OBJ/MTL file containing over 2 million triangles, and rendered using our custom C++ ray tracing engine. The warm canopy lights contrast against a deep twilight sky, while neon pink accents on the pillars and roof edge add a cyberpunk atmosphere. Reflective car paint, chrome, and a wet-look ground plane bring the scene to life with realistic material interactions.

Implementation

Engine Features

Built from the ground up in C++ following the Suffern architecture. Every feature below was implemented for the Homework 5 base and extended for this project.

▲

Triangle Meshes & OBJ Loading

Full OBJ/MTL parser that loads complex meshes with per-face material assignment. Supports coordinate transforms for Blender-to-engine axis mapping.

◉

Primitives

Analytical ray-intersection for Spheres, Planes, and Triangles with proper normal computation and bounding box support.

■

Matte Material

Lambertian diffuse shading using a Diffuse BRDF. Supports ambient and diffuse coefficients with configurable color.

✧

Phong Material

Full Phong illumination model with ambient, diffuse, and specular (Glossy BRDF) components. Configurable exponent controls highlight sharpness.

♦

Reflective Material

Mirror-like reflections via recursive ray tracing with a Specular BRDF. Used for car paint, chrome, and glass with tinted reflection colors.

☼

Point & Directional Lights

Point lights with distance attenuation for the canopy fixtures, and directional lights for moonlight and global fill. 20+ lights in the scene.

◖

Shadow Casting

Shadow rays traced from hit points to every light source. The ShadowCaster tracer integrates shadow tests into the full shading pipeline.

↻

Recursive Reflections

Reflective materials recursively trace reflected rays up to a configurable depth, producing realistic multi-bounce reflections on the car body and ground.

▦

Jittered Anti-Aliasing

Stratified jittered supersampling with n×n sub-pixel rays per pixel. The high-res render uses 6×6 = 36 samples per pixel for smooth edges.

⚙

BVH Acceleration

Bounding Volume Hierarchy partitions 1M+ triangles into a balanced tree for O(log n) ray intersection tests, dramatically cutting render times.

✪

Bloom Post-Processing

Screen-space bloom effect: bright pixels above a threshold are blurred with a separable Gaussian and composited back, creating neon glow on lights and emissive surfaces.

▢

Perspective & Parallel Cameras

Perspective camera with configurable eye position and direction-through-pixel projection. Parallel camera available for orthographic views.

Performance

Acceleration Structure

With over 1 million primitives, brute-force ray intersection is impractical. Our BVH acceleration structure reduces intersection tests from O(n) to O(log n), yielding massive speedups.

Configuration Resolution Samples/px Render Time
Low Res + BVH 480 × 360 2 × 2 ~36 seconds
No Acceleration (brute force) 16 × 12 1 × 1 ~3 mins
High Res + BVH 1920 × 1080 6 × 6 ~1 hr 46 min
High Res Wide Angle + BVH 1920 × 1080 6 × 6 ~1 hr 6 min

Contributions

Javeria

Implemented all utility classes (Point3D, Vector3D, RGBColor, Ray, ShadeInfo, BBox). Implemented geometry classes: Sphere (quadratic formula), Plane (dot product), Triangle (Möller–Trumbore algorithm). Implemented World, Camera, and Plane classes from HW5 with minor tweaks. Implemented the BRDF system: Lambertian Diffuse, Phong Glossy, and Perfect Mirror Specular. Implemented materials: Matte, Phong, and Reflective (with recursive mirror reflections up to depth 5). Implemented the lighting system: abstract Light base class, PointLight, and DirectionalLight with shadow ray support. Implemented the Tracer hierarchy: Basic tracer and ShadowCaster tracer. Implemented BVH acceleration structure reducing intersection tests from O(n) to O(log n). Added cyberpunk neon lighting using Bloom post-processing class.

Umair

Sourced gas station, car, and motorcycle 3D assets from free public sources. Prepared and adjusted all models in Blender (scale, orientation, axis alignment). Exported combined scene as OBJ and identified all 50+ material names from MTL file. Created distinct material definitions mapped to all MTL material names. Extended OBJ loader to support per-material loading via std::map. Determined all light positions by converting Blender coordinates to ray tracer coordinate system. Tuned light sources across multiple iterations for correct scene brightness. Built and maintained the complete build file through all iterations. Implemented Jittered sampler for antialiasing using n×n randomly offset rays per pixel. Built the project website with scene overview, features, acceleration comparison, build file references, contributions, and references. Rendering and performance comparison conducted jointly with Javeria.
